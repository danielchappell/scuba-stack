Product

What shipped, and why it matters

Two people can now edit the same document at the same time and see each other's cursors live, with no save button and no overwrite.

This is the slice that turns the editor from single-player into a tool teams can actually work in together. It is intentionally narrow: text only, two concurrent editors, one document. It proves the hard part works before we widen it.

  • Live presence: named cursors and selections update within ~80ms.
  • No conflicts: simultaneous edits to the same line merge cleanly.
  • Offline-tolerant: edits made while disconnected reconcile on reconnect.
Not yet in scope

Comments, more than two editors, and rich formatting are deferred to the next mandate. The slice is for de-risking sync, not for release.

Architecture

How it works underneath

A CRDT sync layer sits between the editor and a thin relay, so clients converge without a central authority resolving conflicts.

Each client holds the full document state as a conflict-free replicated data type; the relay only fans out encrypted deltas. That keeps the server stateless and makes offline reconnection a merge rather than a special case.

  • CRDT (Yjs-style) document model; deltas, not full-doc syncs.
  • Stateless WebSocket relay; horizontally scalable, no sticky sessions.
  • Presence on a separate ephemeral channel, off the persistence path.
Open risk

Large-document delta history grows unbounded; needs a compaction/snapshot strategy before this scales past the slice. Flagged below.

Needs your decision

Three calls before Review closes

01

Snapshot strategy: server-side compaction or client-driven?

Affects whether the relay stays fully stateless. Architecture team recommends client-driven; wants your sign-off because it shifts a cost to the client.

02

Hold the two-editor cap, or fund the N-editor generalization now?

Going to N now adds ~1 mandate but avoids a rework later. Product lead leans hold; flagging the tradeoff.

03

Is ~80ms presence latency acceptable as the target, or tighter?

Tighter means a dedicated presence region. Cost vs. feel call that's yours, not ours.
